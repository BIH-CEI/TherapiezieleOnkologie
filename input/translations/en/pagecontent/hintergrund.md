### Background

Therapy goals are a core element of shared decision-making in oncology. They describe the **intent** of a treatment plan and are typically chosen jointly by the patient and the treating team. Common categories include:

- **Curative** — aiming for remission / cure
- **Life-prolonging** — extending overall survival when cure is unlikely
- **Symptom control / palliative** — relieving symptoms and burden of disease
- **Quality of life** — preserving or improving the patient's functional status

Documenting these goals in a structured, interoperable form supports continuity of care across institutions, secondary use of routine data, and transparency for patients.
